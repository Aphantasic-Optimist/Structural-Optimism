# Submit Evidence

Help strengthen the framework by submitting peer-reviewed research, experimental findings, or systematic reviews.

## What Evidence We're Looking For

### Primary Research

- **Peer-reviewed studies**: Published in reputable journals
- **Experimental findings**: With clear methodology and results
- **Systematic reviews**: Meta-analyses and comprehensive reviews
- **Replication studies**: Independent verification of key findings

## Evidence Standards

### Required Information

All evidence submissions must include:

1. **Full citation**: Authors, year, title, journal, DOI
2. **Primary source**: Link to original paper (not review/summary)
3. **Key findings**: What was actually observed/measured
4. **Methodology**: How the study was conducted
5. **Relevance**: How this relates to framework claims

### Quality Criteria

We assess evidence using GRADE methodology:

#### Study Quality (Risk of Bias)
- Pre-registration status
- Adequate controls
- Appropriate blinding
- Sufficient sample size
- Proper statistical methods

#### Replication Status (Consistency)
- Independent replication
- Consistent across research groups
- Similar effect sizes
- No major conflicting findings

#### Relevance (Directness)
- Directly tests the claim
- Minimal bridging arguments needed
- Original authors make relevant claims
- Appropriate domain

#### Precision (Effect Size)
- Narrow confidence intervals
- Meaningful effect size
- Adequate statistical power
- Not likely noise

#### Publication Bias
- Negative results published
- No file drawer problem
- Peer-reviewed (not just preprint)
- Unpublished replications available

### What We Don't Accept

- **Non-peer-reviewed sources**: Blog posts, social media, news articles (unless citing primary research)
- **Tertiary sources**: Wikipedia, encyclopedias (use their citations instead)
- **Cherry-picked evidence**: Must include contradictory findings
- **Misrepresented research**: Claims not made by original authors
- **Fabricated connections**: Adding consciousness claims to physics papers that don't make them

## Types of Evidence Contributions

### Supporting Evidence

Evidence that strengthens existing claims:

- Independent replications
- Larger sample sizes
- Better methodology
- Cross-cultural confirmation

### Contradictory Evidence

Evidence that challenges existing claims:

- Failed replications
- Contradictory findings
- Alternative explanations
- Methodological critiques

**Note**: Contradictory evidence is valuable! We actively seek it to maintain rigor.

### Novel Evidence

Evidence for new claims or mechanisms:

- Unexplored domains
- New experimental techniques
- Cross-disciplinary connections
- Emerging research areas

### Meta-Evidence

Evidence about evidence:

- Systematic reviews
- Meta-analyses
- Replication studies
- Methodological critiques

## After Submission

### What to Expect

1. **Acknowledgment**: We'll confirm receipt within 1 week
2. **Review**: We'll assess the evidence (1-4 weeks)
3. **Feedback**: We may ask clarifying questions
4. **Decision**: Accept, revise, or decline with reasoning
5. **Integration**: Accepted evidence is integrated into framework
6. **Recognition**: You're acknowledged as contributor

### Response Times

- **Critical evidence**: 1 week
- **High priority**: 2 weeks
- **Standard**: 4 weeks
- **Low priority**: 8 weeks

### Appeals

If your submission is declined:

1. We'll explain our reasoning
2. You can provide additional context
3. We'll reconsider with new information
4. Final decision is documented
