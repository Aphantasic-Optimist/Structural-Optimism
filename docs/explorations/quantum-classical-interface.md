# Quantum-Classical Interface (L0.5)

!!! danger "Confidence: ★☆☆☆☆ Speculative"
    This is **highly speculative**. There is no direct experimental evidence for this layer in neural tissue.

---

## What This Is

A hypothetical layer where quantum effects transition into classical neural signals.

**The problem it addresses:** Quantum effects operate at femtosecond timescales. Neural processing operates at millisecond timescales. How do they connect?

**The hypothesis:** There exists a mechanism (L0.5) that:

1. Maintains quantum coherence long enough to be functionally relevant
2. Transduces quantum information into classical neural signals
3. Operates in the warm, wet environment of the brain

---

## Why This Matters

If consciousness involves quantum effects (a big "if"), there must be a bridge between:

- **Quantum world:** Superposition, entanglement, coherence (femtoseconds)
- **Classical world:** Neural firing, synaptic transmission (milliseconds)

Without L0.5, quantum consciousness theories have a **timescale problem**.

## The Decoherence Problem

**The challenge:** Quantum coherence is extremely fragile. The brain is:

- **Warm** (~37°C) — Thermal noise destroys coherence
- **Wet** — Water molecules cause rapid decoherence
- **Noisy** — Constant electrochemical activity

**Tegmark's calculation (2000):** Decoherence in neural microtubules would occur in ~10⁻¹³ seconds—far too fast to affect neural processing.

**Counter-arguments:**
- Quantum biology shows coherence CAN persist in warm, wet systems (photosynthesis, bird navigation)
- Tegmark's model may not capture all relevant physics
- Novel mechanisms might shield coherence

**Current status:** The decoherence problem is unresolved. This is a major gap.

## What Would Confirm This

L0.5 would be supported if:

1. **In vivo quantum coherence** — Direct measurement of quantum effects in living neural tissue
2. **Functional relevance** — Showing these effects influence neural computation
3. **Mechanism identified** — Understanding how coherence is maintained
4. **Timescale bridge** — Explaining how femtosecond effects influence millisecond processes

**Upcoming research:** Google Research Awards (2025) is funding development of in vivo quantum probes. Results expected 2026-2027.

---

## What Would Disprove This

L0.5 would be falsified if:

1. **No quantum effects found** — Sensitive in vivo probes show no coherence in neural tissue
2. **Decoherence too fast** — Measured decoherence times are too short for functional relevance
3. **Classical explanation sufficient** — Consciousness fully explained without quantum effects
4. **Mechanism impossible** — Theoretical proof that no mechanism can bridge the timescales
