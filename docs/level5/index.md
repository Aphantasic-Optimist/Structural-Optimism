## Academic Standards

### Our Methodology

**Evidence Classification:**

| Rating | Meaning | Criteria |
|--------|---------|----------|
| ★★★★★ | Established | Multiple replications, peer-reviewed, consistent |
| ★★★★☆ | Strong | Replicated, peer-reviewed, minor inconsistencies |
| ★★★☆☆ | Promising | Initial findings, awaiting replication |
| ★★☆☆☆ | Preliminary | Single study or preprint, speculative mechanism |
| ★☆☆☆☆ | Speculative | Theoretical framework, no direct evidence |

**Source Tiering:**
- Tier 1: Peer-reviewed primary research
- Tier 2: Review articles, textbooks
- Tier 3: Preprints, conference proceedings
- Tier 4: Secondary sources, commentary

**Verification Protocol:**
- Primary source verification
- Replication status check
- Cross-cultural validation
- Falsification criteria
- Counter-evidence review

### Falsification Criteria

**What would prove us wrong:**

1. Large-scale studies showing isolation predicts better health than connection
2. Examples of complexity emerging from pure competition without cooperation
3. Cultures where isolation is universally experienced as flourishing
4. Historical examples of fragmentation outcompeting integration long-term

**We invite evidence that would falsify these claims.**
