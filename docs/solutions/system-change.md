# System Change

**Individual choice matters. Collective action matters more.**

---

## The System Problem

The extraction economy isn't one company or one platform.

It's a **system**—interlocking incentives, technologies, and behaviors that reinforce each other.

- Investors fund growth
- Growth requires engagement
- Engagement requires extraction
- Extraction fragments
- Fragmentation creates more extraction opportunities

**Individual choices can't break this loop alone.**

**But collective choices can.**

---

## What Must Change

### 1. Data Ownership

**Current:** Companies own your data. You have no rights.

**Alternative:** You own your data. Companies must ask permission.

**How:**
- Legal frameworks for data ownership
- Technical infrastructure for personal data vaults
- Economic models that compensate data creators
- Portability rights (take your data when you leave)

### 2. Algorithmic Transparency

**Current:** Algorithms are black boxes. No one knows how they work.

**Alternative:** Algorithms are auditable. Effects are measurable.

**How:**
- Mandatory disclosure of optimization targets
- Independent auditing of algorithmic effects
- User control over recommendation systems
- Public research access to platform data

### 3. Connection-Optimized Design

**Current:** Design optimizes for engagement (time, clicks, shares).

**Alternative:** Design optimizes for wellbeing (connection, understanding, growth).

**How:**
- Regulation of addictive design patterns
- Incentives for connection-positive features
- Measurement of social health, not just engagement
- Design standards that protect attention

### 4. Integration Over Extraction

**Current:** Business models extract value from users.

**Alternative:** Business models create value with users.

**How:**
- Cooperative ownership models
- User-owned platforms
- Subscription models that align incentives
- Public investment in social infrastructure

---

## The Levers

### Regulation

Governments can:
- Require data ownership rights
- Mandate algorithmic transparency
- Ban addictive design patterns
- Break up monopolies
- Fund alternatives

**Challenge:** Regulatory capture, slow pace, jurisdictional limits.

**Opportunity:** Democratic pressure can move governments.

### Markets

Consumers can:
- Choose alternatives
- Pay for non-extractive services
- Boycott extractive platforms
- Invest in aligned companies

**Challenge:** Network effects, switching costs, convenience.

**Opportunity:** Preferences are shifting. Alternatives are emerging.

### Technology

Builders can:
- Create user-owned infrastructure
- Build transparent algorithms
- Design for connection
- Open-source alternatives

**Challenge:** Funding, adoption, network effects.

**Opportunity:** The technology exists. It needs will.

### Culture

Communities can:
- Normalize phone-free spaces
- Celebrate connection over engagement
- Stigmatize extraction
- Model alternatives

**Challenge:** Cultural change is slow.

**Opportunity:** Cultural change is powerful and lasting.

## The Transition Path

### Phase 1: Awareness (Now)

- Understand the extraction economy
- Recognize the mechanisms
- See the alternatives
- Choose differently (phone down, eyes up, smile)

### Phase 2: Alternatives (Emerging)

- Build and use non-extractive platforms
- Support cooperative models
- Create connection-positive spaces
- Demonstrate that alternatives work

### Phase 3: Pressure (Building)

- Demand regulatory change
- Shift market preferences
- Stigmatize extraction
- Celebrate integration

### Phase 4: Transformation (Coming)

- New business models become dominant
- Extraction becomes unacceptable
- Connection becomes the default
- "We" is rebuilt

**Timeline:** Unknown. But direction is clear.
