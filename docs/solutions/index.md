## The Honest Assessment

### What's Established (★★★★★)

- Data centers consume ~1.5% of global electricity, projected to reach 3% by 2030 ([IEA 2024](https://www.iea.org/reports/data-centres-and-data-transmission-networks))
- Social isolation increases mortality by 32% ([Wang et al. 2023](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-023-01617-6), N=2.2M)
- SOLID protocol provides technical foundation for user-owned data ([W3C standards](https://solidproject.org))
- Local LLMs can run on consumer hardware ([Ollama documentation](https://ollama.ai))

### What's Supported (★★★★☆)

- Engagement optimization differs from genuine connection in health outcomes
- Information asymmetry has thermodynamic costs (Landauer's principle)
- Distributed systems can achieve comparable functionality to centralized ones
- Mental health has declined correlating with smartphone adoption

### What's Speculative (★★☆☆☆)

- Specific timeline for transition (2030-2035 estimates)
- Market size projections ($1.6 trillion)
- Adoption curves for alternatives
- Exact energy efficiency gains

### What Could Be Wrong

- Network effects may be stronger than alternatives can overcome
- Regulatory capture may prevent meaningful change
- Users may prefer convenience over ownership
- Transition may be slower or different than projected
